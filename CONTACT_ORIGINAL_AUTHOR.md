## Additional Context & Tips

### What to Include

**Must Include**:
- Your name and contact information
- Project name (Artha) and purpose (Indian market)
- Key features you added
- GPL compliance measures you've taken
- Links to your GitHub repository (once created)
- Appreciation for their work

**Optional but Nice**:
- Link to App Store (if already published)
- Offer to make changes if they have concerns
- Ask if they'd be okay being mentioned as supportive (don't assume)
- Personal touch about why you chose Dime

### What NOT to Do

‚ùå Don't ask for permission (GPL doesn't require it)
‚ùå Don't apologize for forking (it's your right under GPL)
‚ùå Don't make promises you can't keep
‚ùå Don't send before your code is public (look hypocritical)
‚ùå Don't be defensive or confrontational
‚ùå Don't criticize Dime (even constructively) - focus on additions

### Expected Responses

**Best Case**: "This is awesome! Happy to see Dime adapted for India!"
- Thank them warmly
- Ask if they'd like to be mentioned as supportive
- Offer to collaborate if interested

**Neutral Case**: No response or brief acknowledgment
- Totally fine! They're busy
- They don't owe you a response
- Proceed as planned

**Concern Case**: Questions about GPL compliance or attribution
- Respond quickly and thoroughly
- Fix any issues they raise
- Be cooperative and grateful for feedback

**Worst Case**: They're upset (very unlikely given GPL)
- Stay calm and professional
- Point to GPL terms (you have the right)
- Emphasize your compliance efforts
- Offer to discuss and resolve concerns

### After Sending

- Don't spam or follow up too quickly
- Give them at least 1-2 weeks to respond
- If no response after 3 weeks, assume it's okay to proceed
- If they respond positively, thank them and keep them updated
- If they express concerns, address them immediately
